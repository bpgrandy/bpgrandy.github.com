If you’re not happy, ask yourself:

 

  1. 1.Is your environment clean, and are you fostering the relationships around you? Clutter in your environment is reflective of clutter in your mind.


  1. 2.Have you exercised recently? (lack of endorphins by themselves can make you feel miserable) Also, exercise can lead to a cycle of low motivation and willpower. Getting the courage to do the first few workouts will give you the momentum to change your energy into an upward spiral.


  1. 3.Are you doing something with your life you consider worthy? One of the most satisfying paths to happiness is doing something that is greater than yourself. Find some high level values or causes and work towards those. Working with something you truly believe in vs. getting a paycheck can mean night and day with regards to your sense of purpose and happiness.


  1. 4.Are you around people you respect and enjoy? If you find it hard to connect with those around you, keep looking. Understand that human connection can be one of the most difficult things to achieve. If you don’t connect with people, don’t hate on those people. Understand that everyone sees the world differently. Some people you will see eye to eye, some people you will fall in love with, and some people it will seem like you’re speaking an entirely different language.
    http://www.personalitypage.com/html/portraits.html Try not to impose your value filter on others. Everyone is living their lives as best as they see fit. Continue to search for good people.


  1. 5.Are you learning on a daily basis? new ideas and new thoughts foster the human condition.


  1. 6.Is your Maslow’s pyramid covered? http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maslow%27s_hierarchy_of_needs


  1. 7.Are you staying optimistic, being open to new ideas and new people, or falling victim to cynicism?


  1. 8.On a daily basis, do you feel like you have freedom of choice to do what you want, do you feel liberated? Or do you feel trapped? what is causing you to feel trapped?  Can you change that situation?  If not, learn how to best work with it.


  1. 9.Have you cut the bullshit from your life? Tell people what you really think of them, and how you really feel. As long as you are frank, and not emotional when sharing your beliefs about people and the world in general, people will respond openly.  Be open to your friends and family.  Communication is the first step to liberation.  A disconnect between what you feel and how you act can cause constant discontent.


  1. 10.When you have happy moments , or moments that make you laugh, let them flood through your body to the fullest. Positive energy begets positive energy.


  1. 11.Acceptance.  Accept people, accept situations in your life. Love people, try not to judge them.


  1. 12.Don't focus on what you are against but rather what you are for.  Mother Teresa once said "I will never attend an anti-war rally; if you have a peace rally, invite me."  The sub-conscious mind doesn't understand negative thoughts, so by focusing what you are against, your sub-conscious will bring more of that into your life.


  1. 13.Really explore your interests, your environment, and let your mind wonder on a daily basis, you'll be surprised what kind of lessons and epiphanies you take away.  Remember, as said by The Buchaneer Scholar, "most learning is a side effect."


  1. 14.Carpe diem.  Really seize the day.  Don't sit around waiting for life to pass.  Grab it by the bull horns and do as much as you can in these 24 hours we're given each day.  Also, your mind measures the passing of time by “frame changes”, or changes from one situation or environment to the next.  The more frame changes you engage in, the more life you'll feel you are living.


  1. 15.If you are depressed, understand your condition, and that you're not alone. http://www.nytimes.com/2010/02/28/magazine/28depression-t.html There's also a book I've read called the Happiness Hypothesis, which just talks about what creates happiness and how you can have a lifetime of happiness.  Commit your life to something bigger than yourself. Another few books I'd recommend to help the human condition: Consolations of philosophy by Alain de Botton, the first 40 pages of The Power of Now (the rest of the book just kind of repeats itself), and communication books.  Remember, 70% of your success in the world is based on communication, only 30% is skill.  Being a good communicator can completely change your life.


  1. 16.Take care of your body and mind.  I've been playing games at lumosity recently (http://lumosity.com) and have found that it betters my mood, my ability to talk and connect ideas, and my brain in general. Try cutting all drugs out of your life for a couple months. If you’re in a bad situation, drugs and alcohol will only continue to cloud your judgement and stop you from seeing your way out.


  1. 17. Smile and be friendly.  Again, positive energy attracts positive energy, and the same goes for negative.  If you are open and friendly to the people around you, more open and friendly people will come into your life.


  1. 18. Is there anything weighing down on your subconscious?  Thinking about it day and day will just spiral you further into depression.  Decide to solve it, or don't and stop caring.  Remember, not doing things and not taking paths that other people are telling you to take is liberating in it of itself.  Freedom is really one of the biggest contributors to the happiness of the human condition. Life is just a roller coaster ride, just ride it and enjoy it.

  2. 19. Do things for other people. Buy people gifts. Do people favors. If something needs cleaning up and it’s someone else’s mess, just take care of it. Striving towards higher values and a quest to push the limits of how good a person you can be can be a satisfying pursuit. Also helping people and brightening others’ day can give you a sense of purpose and make you forget about your own problems.

  3. 20. Be true to yourself. If you have adopted ways of interacting with certain people because of the way your relationship has developed, but is not really true to yourself, break it. You can work with your own mood and personality as long as you stay positive. Maintaining a continual fascade can weigh down on you and make you feel as if you’re living a lie.


I'll end with this quote from Carl Sagan.  Once of the most liberating things you can realize, is really realize the true insignificance of your daily actions on the outcome of the universe.  I like astronomy, because I think it is very humbling, to realize how we are each simply atoms, getting stressed and upset about which direction to take.  None of it really matters in the end and that should free you to take risks without stressing over the potential hardships.
