fellow sojourners:

break the Chains that Bind

 

Curiosity gives one a natural inclination to explore in the face of darkness. Curiosity is what leads us to learn and be endlessly fascinated by the sciences.

Curiosity is what drives us to explore the moon, the stars, and to wonder and search for other sentient forms of life.

Curiosity is what brings us out a bad relationship, out a dead end job, out of acting in broken failing ways: to hope for a better tomorrow.


Without curiosity man is no better than any other creatures on the planet.

Without that constant yearning for something better we would stay stagnant.

Without hope, faith, or whatever to leap without looking we would never take any risks and avoid a world of prosperity, treasure, and a better life for all.


There are many things that stop us from reaching our true potential as individual souls:


fears, prejudices, pride, our sense of identity/ego, fear of being vulnerable, what other people think your life should be, what are the realities of today, what baggage from the past you carry with you, your ever decreasing sense of self-worth based on how you have performed relative to your peers in the past...


Today, I ask you to leave that all behind.

Today, I ask you to walk forward into the darkness with nothing more than your heart doing something you passionately believe in.

Today, I ask you to explore forward not knowing where the path will lead and trust that with good intentions everything will work out.


Finding your bearing is much harder than taking the first step so make sure you head in the right direction: for your reasons; no one else’s.


Inexhaustible passion and drive are much greater resources than experience, knowledge, or aptitude. All that will come with drive.


Mountains are created one rock at a time, so don’t dismay if you start with a small hill.


One day you’ll look back wondering how you ever lived someone else’s life, trapped in a box.


Be true to yourself.

Believe in yourself.

Don’t give up on yourself.


Don’t live someone else’s life.

Be an active director of your life, not a passive effect to others’ will.

Take people’s feedback respectfully, but with a grain of salt.


Now/today/...eventually:

 

Go somewhere new.

Try something different.

Explore a new place, whether physical, virtual, in your mind, or in your heart.

Break the bonds of old habits of thinking.
